Investigation Overview¶

My goal is to discover the most important features that affects on the amount of carbon dioxide emissions from cars.

Dataset Overview¶

This dataset captures the details of how CO2 emissions by a vehicle can vary with the different features. The dataset has been taken from Canada Government official open data website. This is a compiled version. This contains data over a period of 7 years. There are total 7385 rows and 12 columns. There are few abbreviations that has been used to describe the features. I am listing them out in the next slide.

Model 4WD/4X4 = Four-wheel drive AWD = All-wheel drive FFV = Flexible-fuel vehicle SWB = Short wheelbase LWB = Long wheelbase EWB = Extended wheelbase

Transmission A = Automatic AM = Automated manual AS = Automatic with select shift AV = Continuously variable M = Manual 3 - 10 = Number of gears

Fuel type X = Regular gasoline Z = Premium gasoline D = Diesel E = Ethanol (E85) N = Natural gas

Fuel Consumption City and highway fuel consumption ratings are shown in litres per 100 kilometres (L/100 km) - the combined rating (55% city, 45% hwy) is shown in L/100 km and in miles per gallon (mpg)

CO2 Emissions The tailpipe emissions of carbon dioxide (in grams per kilometre) for combined city and highway driving.

(Relation between car make and Co2 emissions)¶

We can observe that most of the costly brand cars like BUGATTI and LAMBORGHINI are emmitting lot of carbon emissions and brands like HONDA and FIAT are least carbon emitting vehicles.

(Relation between engine size and Co2 emissions)¶

We can observe that the larger the engine, the higher emissions of Co2.

We can see that Large size engines consumes more fuel and emitt large amount of carbon emissions.

(Relation between number of cylinders and Co2 emissions)¶

we can observe that the higher the number of cylinders, the higher the CO2 emissions.

Correlation Matrix¶

Fuel Consumption Comb (mpg) is negatively correlated to all the features. Both Fuel Consumption City (L/100 km) and Fuel Consumption Hwy (L/100 km) have very strong positive correlation of 0.99 and 0.98 with Fuel Consumption Comb (L/100 km), since Fuel Consumption Comb (L/100 km) is redundant. CO2 Emissions(g/km) has highest positive correlation of 0.92 with Fuel Consumption City (L/100 km) and Fuel Consumption Comb (L/100 km) and negative correlation of -0.91 with Fuel Consumption Comb (mpg).
